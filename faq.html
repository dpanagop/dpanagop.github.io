Who is Dimitrios Panagopoulos?

Who is Dimitrios Panagopoulos?

Dimitrios Panagopoulos is a Data Science Leader and PhD Mathematician based in Athens, Greece. He works as a Manager at EY Greece in the Data Analytics & AI practice and serves as a part-time Lecturer of Machine Learning and AI at a joint postgraduate programme in Business Mathematics run by the National and Kapodistrian University of Athens and the Athens University of Economics and Business.

What is his academic background?

He holds a PhD in Mathematics (February 2009), an MSc in Pure Mathematics (November 2003), and a degree in Mathematics (September 1999), all from the Department of Mathematics of the National and Kapodistrian University of Athens. His doctoral and postdoctoral research focused on algebraic topology, group theory, homological algebra, and cryptography.

Has he founded any companies?

Yes. Between 2013 and 2016 he founded Eureka Module, a data science startup focused on analytics and consulting. As Lead Data Scientist he built recommender systems for retail clients and delivered customer segmentation projects using Python, Hadoop, and machine learning techniques.

What teaching experience does he have?

Beyond his current lectureship in the Business Mathematics MSc programme, he has taught mathematics at the University of Ioannina, the Agricultural University of Athens, the Technological Educational Institute (TEI) of Messolonghi, and the TEI of Peloponnese. He also spent over a decade as a freelance mathematics tutor (2008–2016).

What does he specialise in?

What is his expertise in Generative AI?

At EY he has managed teams building graph-enhanced RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) systems, agentic due diligence tools, and early warning systems that use GenAI to process news for banking clients. He has also participated in AI lifecycle strategy and AI system reviews for global NGOs, and teaches GenAI concepts in his academic lectureship.

What is his experience with Graph Data Science and Neo4j?

He is a Neo4j Certified Professional (certified September 2023) and has hands-on experience building graph-enhanced systems in production consulting environments. He writes about Graph Data Science — including Neo4j applications, anomaly detection, and clustering — on his Medium profile.

What is Topological Data Analysis and how does he apply it?

Topological Data Analysis (TDA) uses tools from algebraic topology — such as persistent homology — to analyse the shape of data. Dimitrios has published academic work connecting TDA with clustering algorithms and lazy learning, and has applied these ideas to practical data science problems. His 2022 arXiv paper "Topological data analysis and clustering" is the most accessible entry point to this work.

What industries has he worked in?

His consulting work at EY has focused primarily on financial services — including major Greek and European banks — covering projects in credit risk, customer segmentation, non-performing loan management, hard collection systems, loyalty analytics, and digital channel optimisation. He has also worked with a global shipping company and international NGOs on AI strategy and governance.

What technical tools and languages does he use?

His core technical stack includes Python, R, SQL, and Neo4j for data science and graph work; PySpark, Hadoop, Hive, and Databricks for big data; Azure for cloud; and Power BI and SPSS Modeler for analytics and visualisation. He holds an Azure Fundamentals certification (Microsoft, February 2024) and a Certified Analytics Professional (CAP) credential from INFORMS, recertified in October 2024.
